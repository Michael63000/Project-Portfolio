Jan. 2020 - Dec. 2023

This was my Master's of Applied Science project, by far my biggest project. The project involved researching current hip prosthesis technology, conceptualizing and designing a novel hip joint that addressed research gaps, and prototyping and testing the hip joint. Novelties of this project were that the design contained a four-bar linkage interfacing with an actuator to power leg motion. Market hip disarticulation prostheses do not make use of an actuator. I am thankful for my thesis supervisors Dr. Edward Lemaire and Dr. Natalie Baddour for mentoring my progression during this project.

Sept. 2020 - Dec. 2020

My partner and I conducted online interviews of leaders in the field of prosthetic and orthotic design. From their insights, we created a blog presenting social and functional implications in this field: cosmetics for public acceptance, means to return to favorite activities, comfort, and affordability.

Personal contributions: local prosthetist (Hossein) interview and write-up, merging diagrams and themes with partner's interview outcomes

Prosthetics and Orthotics: Design Considerations and the State-of-the-Art

Jan. 2020 - Sept. 2020

Our team of four investigated methods of improving the experience of young caregivers when caring for people living with dementia. The project was carried out in partnership with the Dementia Society of Ottawa and Renfrew County (DSORC). By means of a market analysis of available support tools, and an online survey of challenges regarding caregiving at a young age, we proposed an all-in-one stop hub for support resources, and presented our results to DSORC, increasing awareness of such resources.

Personal contributions: literature review of dementia medical condition, team interviews with DSORC director, caregiver survey brainstorming and result interpretation, team presentation and documentation of caregiver recommendations

Presentation at the 2020 READi Symposium

Sept. 2019 - Dec. 2019

In a team of four, we conceptualized a passive hip exoskeleton that provided augmenting moments to assist elderly gait. The project contained a fully parametrized SolidWorks model and a MATLAB graphical user interface that dimensions the design based on the user's height and weight.

Personal contributions: literature review of existing technology, ratchet-paw system conceptualization with team, SolidWorks modelling with team, MATLAB calculations (frontal plane dynamics and shaft analysis), MATLAB-SolidWorks parametrization, engineering drawings with team

Sept. 2019 - Dec. 2019

In a team of five, we designed a hip prosthesis for total hip replacement surgery. The prosthesis contained original components that were compatible with the human body and withstood the largest hip contact forces seen in gait.

Personal contributions: critical review of current hip implants, motion analysis, SolidWorks modelling, renders and drawings

Jan. 2019 - Apr. 2019

My project partner and I built a functioning servo-motor actuated EMG prosthetic hand. This hand used speed proportional control: the speed at which the hand opens/closes correlated directly to the amplitude of the electrical signal coming from the muscles in the forearm.

Personal contributions: algorithm design with partner, prototyping of open-source design, code debugging and testing with partner, video creation

EMG Prosthesis Prototype Video

Apr. 2019

I created a MATLAB simulator that determined the temperature profile of a femoral implant curing onto the bone during total hip replacement surgery. This temperature profile informed potential physiological risks due to high curing temperatures.

Jan. 2019 - Mar. 2019

I worked in a team of three people to create a controlled and repeatable experiment that determined the effect of blood viscosity on the volumetric efficiency of a peristaltic pump, the type of pump used in dialysis machines.

Personal contributions: brainstorming of experimental setup (including blood-mimicking fluid), fluid mechanics calculations, running a third of experiments, video creation

Peristaltic Pump Experiment Video

June 2018 - July 2018

We, as a team of four, designed a 5-horsepower gear reducer that is used to provide a large enough torque to open the door of a drawbridge castle.

Personal contributions: gear selection brainstorming, shaft analysis, CAD modelling, engineering drawings

Sept. 2017 - Dec. 2017

My two teammates and I designed and built a 3d printed mechanical prosthetic hand that extended to approach an object and grasped the object once in range. The prototype utilized a proximity sensor to detect the object and and force sensors at the fingertips to control the hand's grasp.

Personal contributions: conceptualization with team, hand CAD modelling and 3D printing, prototype construction with team, code debugging with team, video creation

Personal contributions: balanced efforts with teammate in part sourcing, code debugging, testing, documentation and video creation
